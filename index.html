Dragon Ball Anime

Welcome to Dragon Ball Anime

Your ultimate source for all things Dragon Ball!

Dragon Ball is a Japanese anime series based on the manga of the same name written and illustrated by Akira Toriyama. The series has become one of the most popular and influential anime franchises of all time, known for its epic battles, unique characters, and intricate storyline. The franchise began with the original "Dragon Ball" series, followed by "Dragon Ball Z," "Dragon Ball GT," "Dragon Ball Super," and various movies and specials.

The Dragon Ball anime series is a cornerstone of the anime world, with its blend of action, humor, and deep lore captivating audiences for decades. Its legacy continues to grow, inspiring new generations of fans and creators alike.

Impact and Legacy

Cultural Influence

"Dragon Ball" has significantly impacted global pop culture, influencing countless anime, video games, and other media. Characters like Goku have become cultural icons.

Merchandise

The franchise has spawned a vast range of merchandise, including action figures, clothing, video games, and more.

Fandom

The series boasts a dedicated and diverse fanbase, with conventions, fan art, and fan fiction contributing to its ongoing popularity.

Fun Facts

Creator

Akira Toriyama initially based Goku's character on Sun Wukong from the Chinese novel "Journey to the West."

Transformations

The concept of Saiyan transformations, especially the Super Saiyan form, has become one of the most iconic elements in anime.

Global Reach

"Dragon Ball" has been dubbed in numerous languages and broadcast in over 80 countries.

About the creator

Akira Toriyama(1955-2024)

Akira Toriyama was a Japanese manga artist and character designer. He first achieved mainstream recognition for creating the popular manga series Dr. Slump, before going on to create Dragon Ball (his most famous work) and acting as a character designer for several popular video games such as the Dragon Quest series, Chrono Trigger, and Blue Dragon. Toriyama came to be regarded as one of the most important authors in the history of manga with his works highly influential and popular, particularly Dragon Ball, which many manga artists cite as a source of inspiration.

He earned the 1981 Shogakukan Manga Award for best shōnen/shōjo manga with Dr. Slump, and it went on to sell over 35 million copies in Japan. It was adapted into a successful anime series, with a second anime created in 1997, 13 years after the manga ended.

His next series, Dragon Ball, would become one of the most popular and successful manga in the world. Having sold 260 million copies worldwide, it is one of the best-selling manga series of all time and is considered to be one of the main reasons for the period when manga circulation was at its highest in the mid-1980s and mid-1990s. Overseas, Dragon Ball's anime adaptations have been more successful than the manga and are credited with boosting anime's popularity in the Western world. In 2019, Toriyama was decorated a Chevalier of the French Ordre des Arts et des Lettres for his contributions to the arts.

Episodes

Main Series

    Dragon Ball (1986-1989)

    Synopsis

    The story begins with a young boy named Goku who embarks on a journey to find the seven Dragon Balls, magical orbs that summon a wish-granting dragon when gathered. Along the way, Goku meets many friends and foes, undergoes rigorous training, and participates in martial arts tournaments.

    Key Characters

    Goku, Bulma, Master Roshi, Krillin, Yamcha.

    Dragon Ball Z (1989-1996)

    Synopsis

    The sequel to "Dragon Ball," this series follows Goku into adulthood as he defends Earth against powerful villains. Major arcs include the Saiyan Saga, Frieza Saga, Cell Saga, and Majin Buu Saga. "Dragon Ball Z" introduced the concept of Super Saiyan transformations and expanded the universe significantly.

    Key Characters

    Goku, Vegeta, Gohan, Piccolo, Frieza, Cell, Majin Buu.

    Dragon Ball GT (1996-1997)

    Synopsis

    A non-canon continuation of the story, "Dragon Ball GT" sees Goku transformed into a child and traveling through space to find the Black Star Dragon Balls. Though it was not based on Toriyama's manga, it includes new transformations like Super Saiyan 4.

    Key Characters

    Goku, Pan, Trunks, Baby, Super 17.

    Dragon Ball Super (2015-2018)

    Synopsis

    A direct sequel to "Dragon Ball Z," this series explores new adventures and introduces gods and multiverses. Key story arcs include the Battle of Gods, Resurrection 'F', Universe 6, Future Trunks, and the Tournament of Power.

    Key Characters

    Goku, Vegeta, Beerus, Whis, Zeno, Jiren.

    Dragon Ball Heroes (2018-present)

    Synopsis

    Based on the arcade game of the same name, this promotional anime features alternate storylines and non-canon battles. It introduces new characters and transformations, focusing on the Time Patrol and the fight against the Dark Empire.

    Key Characters

    Goku (Xeno), Vegeta (Xeno), Fu, Cumber.
